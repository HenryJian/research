# Shiitake Farm-Gate Market Size Source Inventory: North America

Checked on 2026-04-29.

Purpose: identify research reports, government tables, extension articles, slides, or market articles that can support a North American shiitake **farm-gate / first-sale** market-size estimate. The main question is not consumer retail value, but value received by growers or first handlers.

## Bottom Line

The best direct public source I found is the **USDA NASS annual Mushrooms report**, which reports U.S. shiitake volume, price, and value of sales at first-sale / grower level.

For Canada, I found no public table that releases shiitake-only farm-gate value by Canada or Ontario. Statistics Canada appears to collect shiitake quantity and value on the Mushroom Growers' Survey questionnaire, but the public table aggregates mushroom sales by province and does not publish species-level shiitake values.

I did not find a reliable Mexico shiitake farm-gate market-size source in public search results.

## Direct Farm-Gate / First-Sale Sources

### 1. USDA NASS, Mushrooms annual reports

Usefulness: **High** for the United States. This is the closest match to "farm gate market size" because it reports specialty mushroom volume, price, and value of sales by type, including shiitake.

Most recent direct data found:

| Crop year | Shiitake volume sold | Price | Value of sales |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2019/20 | 7.013 million lb | US$3.44/lb | US$24.091 million |
| 2020/21 | 7.210 million lb | US$3.44/lb | US$24.836 million |
| 2021/22 | 7.749 million lb | US$3.51/lb | US$27.162 million |
| 2022/23 | 5.652 million lb | US$3.82/lb | US$21.589 million |
| 2023/24 | 3.832 million lb | US$4.92/lb | US$18.864 million |
| 2024/25 | 5.341 million lb | US$6.23/lb | US$33.286 million |

Six-year takeaway: U.S. shiitake first-sale value has generally been a **US$20 million to US$33 million** market since 2019/20, averaging about **US$25.0 million/year** over 2019/20 through 2024/25. The 2024/25 value jumped mainly because the reported grower price increased to **US$6.23/lb**.

Source:

- [USDA NASS, Mushrooms, August 2025 PDF](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Publications/Todays_Reports/reports/mush0825.pdf)
- [USDA NASS / Cornell ESMIS text copy, Mushrooms, August 2025](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/r781wg03d/cn69p2568/x633h081v/mush0825.txt)

Useful older annual reports:

- [USDA NASS / Cornell ESMIS, Mushrooms, August 2024 PDF](https://downloads.usda.library.cornell.edu/usda-esmis/files/r781wg03d/9593wm52z/9306vq05c/mush0824.pdf)
- [USDA NASS / Cornell ESMIS, Mushrooms, August 2023 PDF](https://downloads.usda.library.cornell.edu/usda-esmis/files/r781wg03d/kp78j124b/g158d0210/mush0823.pdf)
- [USDA NASS, Mushrooms, August 2022 PDF](https://www.nass.usda.gov/Publications/Todays_Reports/reports/mush0822.pdf)

Important caveats:

- This is **United States**, not Canada.
- NASS crop years run July-June, not calendar year.
- Reports say they cover growers with at least 200 natural wood logs or commercial indoor growing area, so very small growers may be outside scope.
- NASS values are "value of sales" for the reported crop, which is the right conceptual match for farm-gate / first-sale value but not retail value.

### 2. USDA ERS, Vegetables and Pulses Outlook, December 2025

Source:

- [USDA ERS, Vegetables and Pulses Outlook: December 2025](https://www.ers.usda.gov/sites/default/files/_laserfiche/outlooks/113612/VGS-377.pdf?v=34308)

### 4. Statistics Canada, Mushroom Growers' Survey questionnaire

Usefulness: **High for confirming that species-level shiitake data is collected; low for public values.**

The 2024 questionnaire asks growers to report production and value for:

- shiitake mushrooms,
- oyster mushrooms,
- other specialty mushrooms,
- brown mushrooms,
- white button mushrooms,

Source:

## Indirect / Partial Sources

### 5. Statistics Canada article: A look at the mushroom industry

Usefulness: **Medium for Canadian specialty-mushroom context; not shiitake-only.**

The article uses unpublished Mushroom Growers' Survey data and explicitly discusses farm gate sales values, production volumes, and prices. It says Agaricus mushrooms represented about **98%** of Canadian mushroom production in 2017, with specialty mushrooms including shiitake, oyster, and other varieties making up the remainder. It also gives a 2017 specialty mushroom price of **C$11.88/kg** and says the average specialty mushroom business sold **26,000 kg** and recorded **C$297,077** in sales.

Source:

- [Statistics Canada, A look at the mushroom industry](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/21-004-x/2019001/article/00001-eng.htm)

Why it matters:

- It supports the idea that Canada's specialty mushroom production is a very small share of total mushroom output.
- It is Canada-specific and farm-gate oriented, but not Ontario-specific and not shiitake-specific.

### 6. Cornell Small Farms specialty mushroom industry overview

Usefulness: **Medium.** This is a clear extension-style article summarizing USDA NASS specialty mushroom sales and buyer-demand evidence.

Source:

- [Cornell Small Farms, Defining the Specialty Mushroom Industry](https://smallfarms.cornell.edu/resources/specialty-mushroom-cultivation/defining-the-specialty-mushroom-industry/)

Useful data points:

- Cites USDA-NASS 2018 and says shiitake and oyster are the second and third most-produced mushrooms in the United States.
- Says U.S. specialty mushroom production in 2017 was **25.4 million lb** with **US$96.2 million** in sales value.
- Cites buyer-demand work showing Northeast buyers needed more locally produced shiitake than was available.

Why it matters:

- Good narrative source for specialty mushroom demand and farm-scale opportunity.
- It is not a replacement for USDA NASS when estimating farm-gate value.

### 7. Cornell Small Farms economic articles on log-grown shiitake

### 8. Academic article: Competitive market analysis of the U.S. shiitake marketplace

Usefulness: **Medium.** This is a historical U.S. shiitake producer survey and market-structure article.

Citation:

- Gold, Michael A., Mihaela M. Cernusca, and Larry D. Godsey. 2008. "A Competitive Market Analysis of the United States Shiitake Mushroom Marketplace." HortTechnology 18(3):489-499. DOI: 10.21273/HORTTECH.18.3.489.

Sources:

- [HortTechnology article page](https://journals.ashs.org/view/journals/horttech/18/3/article-p489.xml)
- [University of Missouri Center for Agroforestry PDF](https://centerforagroforestry.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/shiitakeanalysis.pdf)

Why it matters:

- It reports a nationwide 2006 survey of U.S. shiitake producers.
- It gives farm and channel price ranges, including wholesale, farmers market, chef, retail, and on-farm prices.
- It cites USDA NASS 2006 first-sale shiitake grower price at **US$3.24/lb**, close to 2005's **US$3.23/lb**.
- It is old, but still useful for understanding why small log-grown shiitake can sell above commodity first-sale averages.

### 12. USDA AMS Federal Register organic mushroom standards

Usefulness: **Medium for official market context; low for shiitake-only.**

Sources:

- [USDA AMS proposed rule, National Organic Program; Market Development for Mushrooms and Pet Food, March 2024](https://regulations.justia.com/regulations/fedreg/2024/03/11/2024-04973.html)
- [USDA AMS final rule, December 2024](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2024-12-23/pdf/2024-30211.pdf)

Why it matters:

- The proposed rule summarizes the 2021/22 U.S. mushroom crop as **702 million lb** and **US$1.02 billion** in sales and says Agaricus accounted for about **97%** of volume and **93%** of value.
- The final rule updates the context to 2023/24, citing **659 million lb** and **US$1.09 billion** in sales, with Agaricus at about **98%** of volume and **92%** of value.
- This confirms from a regulatory source that specialty mushrooms are a small but higher-value share of the U.S. mushroom market.

Usefulness: **Low-to-medium.** This is an industry article, not a survey table, but it gives a useful older Canadian industry estimate.

Source:

- [Canadian Packaging, Economies Of Scale, May 2014 cover story](https://www.canadianpackaging.com/features/economies-scale-canadian-packaging-may-2014-cover-story/)

Why it matters:

- Treat this as directional only. Later Statistics Canada analysis suggests specialty mushrooms were closer to **2%** of Canadian production volume in 2017.

## Market-Research Pages Found But Not Recommended For Farm-Gate Sizing

I found several commercial "shiitake mushroom market" and "mushroom market" pages from market-research firms. These are not good farm-gate sources because they usually estimate global or regional retail/ingredient market revenue, do not separate fresh farm-gate value, and often hide assumptions behind paid reports.

Use them only for high-level demand narrative, not for Ontario farm planning:

- [Mordor Intelligence, Shiitake Mushroom Market](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/shiitake-mushroom-market)
- [Fortune Business Insights, Shiitake Mushroom Market](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/shiitake-mushroom-market-106571)
- [Data Bridge Market Research, North America Shiitake Mushroom Market](https://www.databridgemarketresearch.com/reports/north-america-shiitake-mushroom-market)

## Sources I Could Not Find

I did not find a public source that directly reports:

- Ontario shiitake farm-gate value,
- Canada shiitake farm-gate value,
- Mexico shiitake farm-gate value,
- North America combined shiitake farm-gate value.

The strongest defensible North American farm-gate estimate should therefore start with USDA NASS U.S. shiitake first-sale value, then treat Canada and Mexico separately as gaps or modeled add-ons.

## Implication For Our Ontario Estimate

My prior Ontario estimate relied too heavily on U.S. retail species-share data. The better evidence hierarchy is:

1. Use USDA NASS shiitake first-sale values to understand U.S. farm-gate scale directly.
2. Use Statistics Canada Ontario all-mushroom farm-gate value as the Ontario base.
3. Use StatCan's specialty-mushroom share / Agaricus dominance and known Ontario grower production as constraints.
4. Use retail species-share data only as a secondary cross-check.

This source inventory suggests the Ontario shiitake farm-gate estimate should be rebuilt more cautiously and probably presented as a scenario range rather than a single confident midpoint.
