# Ontario Shiitake Mushroom Farm-Gate Market Size Estimate

Checked on 2026-04-29. Latest public Statistics Canada mushroom crop year used here: 2024.

> Revision note, 2026-04-29: Treat this estimate as **provisional**. A follow-up source scan found that USDA NASS publishes direct U.S. shiitake first-sale values, while Statistics Canada does not publish Ontario shiitake value separately. Before using this number for planning, rebuild the estimate using [shiitake-farm-gate-source-inventory.md](shiitake-farm-gate-source-inventory.md).

## Bottom Line

I estimate the annual **farm-gate sales value of Ontario-grown shiitake mushrooms** at about **C$20 million**, with a practical planning range of **C$15 million to C$27 million per year**.

| Item | Working estimate |
|---|---:|
| Annual Ontario shiitake farm-gate value | **C$20 million** |
| Practical planning range | **C$15 million to C$27 million** |
| Central implied fresh-weight volume | **about 2.3 million lb/year** |
| Broad volume sensitivity range | **about 1.7 million to 3.0 million lb/year** |
| Implied average farm-gate price | **about C$8 to C$10/lb** |
| Confidence | **Low-to-medium** |

This is an estimate, not a reported statistic. I interpret "farm-gate value" as the value received by Ontario growers for shiitake grown in Ontario, before retail or restaurant markup, regardless of whether the mushrooms are later sold in Ontario or shipped to buyers elsewhere in Canada.

## Why This Has To Be Estimated

Statistics Canada publishes total mushroom production and sales value by province, but the public table does not break Ontario's value out by species. The survey questionnaire does ask growers for shiitake quantity and shiitake sales value, so the data is collected, but the public release is aggregated.

The best available approach is therefore to anchor on Ontario's total mushroom farm-gate value, then estimate shiitake's share using specialty-mushroom benchmarks and known Ontario grower signals.

## Hard Anchors

### Ontario's total mushroom farm-gate base

Statistics Canada reported the following for Ontario mushrooms in 2024:

| Metric | 2024 value |
|---|---:|
| Total production | **84,871 short tons** |
| Total production in pounds | **169.7 million lb** |
| Total value, fresh and processed | **C$468.783 million** |
| Value sold fresh | **C$461.300 million** |
| Implied all-mushroom average value | **about C$2.76/lb** |

Source: [Statistics Canada Table 32-10-0356-01, Area, production and sales of mushrooms](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/t1/tbl1/en/tv.action?pid=3210035601). The same 2024 release states that Ontario sold **84,871 short tons** and accounted for **51.8%** of Canadian mushroom production: [Statistics Canada, Mushroom Growers' Survey, 2024](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/250625/dq250625e-eng.htm).

### Specialty mushrooms are a small but premium part of the market

Statistics Canada's 2023 release says Agaricus mushrooms typically represent about **98%** of Canadian annual mushroom production, with specialty mushrooms such as shiitake, oyster, and other varieties making up the remainder. Source: [Statistics Canada, Mushroom Growers' Survey, 2023](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/240627/dq240627e-eng.htm).

For the species mix and price premium, the best public benchmark I found is the Mushroom Council / Circana U.S. retail specialty report for the 52 weeks ending 2025-11-02:

| Metric | U.S. retail benchmark |
|---|---:|
| Specialty mushrooms share of total fresh mushroom dollars | **7.0%** |
| Specialty mushrooms share of total fresh mushroom pounds | **2.2%** |
| Shiitake share of specialty mushroom dollars | **64.8%** |
| Shiitake share of specialty mushroom pounds | **60.6%** |
| Shiitake retail price | **US$14.94/lb** |

Source: [Mushroom Council, Specialty Mushroom Sales and Performance Review 2025](https://www.mushroomcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialty-Mushroom-Sales-and-Performance-Review-2025.pdf).

This is U.S. retail data, not Ontario farm-gate data. I use it only as a proxy for the relative size and price premium of shiitake within the mushroom category.

### Ontario has at least one very large shiitake producer

Holburne Mushroom Farm in Queensville says it produces about **25,000 lb/week of organic shiitake**, or about **1.3 million lb/year**. It also says its shiitake and oyster mushrooms are distributed through retailers and foodservice partners across Canada. Sources: [Holburne Mushroom Farm - Our Farm](https://www.holburnemushroom.ca/our-farm) and [Holburne Mushroom Farm - Foodservice & Retail](https://www.holburnemushroom.ca/foodservice-retail).

This makes a very low Ontario shiitake farm-gate estimate unlikely. One Ontario grower alone appears large enough to account for more than half of the midpoint volume estimate.

## Estimate Logic

### Method 1: Value-share method

Use the U.S. specialty retail structure as a proxy for shiitake's share of the total mushroom category:

`7.0% specialty dollar share x 64.8% shiitake share of specialty dollars = 4.5% shiitake share of total fresh mushroom dollars`

Then apply that share to Ontario's 2024 fresh mushroom farm-gate value:

`C$461.3 million x 4.5% = C$20.9 million`

This supports a midpoint of about **C$21 million**.

For a range, I used the same Mushroom Council report's U.S. regional specialty shares:

| Scenario | Specialty share | Shiitake share of specialty | Applied to Ontario fresh value | Result |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Lower case, similar to U.S. Great Lakes specialty share | 4.8% | 64.8% | C$461.3M | **C$14.3M** |
| Midpoint, U.S. total specialty share | 7.0% | 64.8% | C$461.3M | **C$20.9M** |
| Upper case, similar to U.S. Northeast specialty share | 9.1% | 64.8% | C$461.3M | **C$27.2M** |

Rounded, this gives a practical Ontario planning range of **C$15 million to C$27 million**.

### Method 2: Volume-price cross-check

The same U.S. retail report shows shiitake at about **4.4 million lb** out of **324.4 million lb** of total fresh mushroom volume:

`4.4M lb / 324.4M lb = 1.36%`

Apply that to Ontario's 2024 mushroom production:

`169.7M lb x 1.36% = about 2.3M lb of shiitake`

Then estimate a farm-gate shiitake price by applying the shiitake retail premium to Ontario's all-mushroom farm-gate average:

`US$14.94/lb shiitake retail price / about US$4.47/lb all-mushroom retail average = about 3.3x premium`

`C$2.76/lb Ontario all-mushroom farm-gate average x 3.3 = about C$9.20/lb`

Volume-price result:

`2.3M lb x C$9.20/lb = about C$21M`

This independently lands very close to the value-share method.

## Operational Meaning For Our Farm

For planning purposes, every **1,000 lb/week** of steady shiitake sales is about **52,000 lb/year**. At a farm-gate price of **C$8 to C$10/lb**, that is roughly:

| Weekly shiitake sales | Annual volume | Annual farm-gate revenue |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 1,000 lb/week | 52,000 lb/year | C$416,000 to C$520,000 |
| 2,500 lb/week | 130,000 lb/year | C$1.04M to C$1.30M |
| 5,000 lb/week | 260,000 lb/year | C$2.08M to C$2.60M |
| 10,000 lb/week | 520,000 lb/year | C$4.16M to C$5.20M |

At the midpoint estimate of **C$20 million**, a farm selling **5,000 lb/week** would represent roughly **10% to 13%** of the estimated Ontario-grown shiitake farm-gate market by value.

## Caveats

- This is **Ontario-grown farm-gate value**, not Ontario consumer demand. Ontario farms may sell product through national distributors or outside Ontario.
- The public Statistics Canada table does not publish Ontario shiitake value directly, so the estimate depends on proxy shares.
- The Mushroom Council / Circana data is U.S. retail data and excludes ethnic and organic store formats, which may matter for shiitake.
- Farm-gate price varies by channel. Direct restaurant, farmers market, distributor, supermarket, organic, sliced, and utility-grade shiitake will not all receive the same price.
- Dried shiitake is not modeled separately. The estimate is intended mainly for fresh-market planning.
