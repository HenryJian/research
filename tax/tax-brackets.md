# 2025 Federal and Ontario Personal Income Tax Brackets

This note summarizes the `2025` personal income tax marginal rates that apply to Ontario residents for quick reference.

Scope notes:

- The rates below are based on `taxable income`.
- This note summarizes only federal tax rates and Ontario provincial tax rates.
- The main bracket tables below do not include `Ontario surtax`, `Ontario Health Premium`, CPP, EI, or other items. `Ontario surtax` is summarized separately in the notes because it does not map cleanly to a single taxable-income bracket.
- If farm income is reported on a personal return, such as through a sole proprietorship, this note can be used as a quick reference. It does not apply to corporate tax filing.

## 2025 Federal Personal Income Tax Marginal Rates

Effective `July 1, 2025`, the lowest federal tax rate was reduced from `15%` to `14%`. Because the change took effect halfway through the year, the first federal bracket for the full `2025` tax year is `14.5%` on a prorated basis.

| Taxable income | Federal rate |
|---|---:|
| `0 – 57,375` | `14.5%` |
| `57,375 – 114,750` | `20.5%` |
| `114,750 – 177,882` | `26%` |
| `177,882 – 253,414` | `29%` |
| `253,414+` | `33%` |

## 2025 Ontario Personal Income Tax Marginal Rates

| Taxable income | Ontario rate |
|---|---:|
| `0 – 52,886` | `5.05%` |
| `52,886 – 105,775` | `9.15%` |
| `105,775 – 150,000` | `11.16%` |
| `150,000 – 220,000` | `12.16%` |
| `220,000+` | `13.16%` |

### Ontario Surtax Marginal Effect

For `2025`, Ontario surtax is:

- `20%` of `Ontario basic tax payable` over `$5,710`
- plus an additional `36%` of `Ontario basic tax payable` over `$7,307`

That means surtax acts as a multiplier on the current Ontario bracket rate rather than as its own taxable-income bracket:

| Current Ontario bracket rate | Extra Ontario marginal rate in first surtax band | Extra Ontario marginal rate after second surtax threshold |
|---|---:|---:|
| `5.05%` | `1.01%` | `2.83%` |
| `9.15%` | `1.83%` | `5.12%` |
| `11.16%` | `2.23%` | `6.25%` |
| `12.16%` | `2.43%` | `6.81%` |
| `13.16%` | `2.63%` | `7.37%` |

In other words, once surtax applies, the effective Ontario marginal rate becomes about `120%` of the nominal Ontario rate in the first surtax band, and about `156%` of the nominal Ontario rate after the second surtax threshold.

## Notes for Use

- The table above is a reference for the nominal federal plus provincial bracket rates. It is not the same as the final effective combined marginal tax rate.
- `Tax saved if the full bracket is sheltered` is estimated using `federal rate + Ontario rate` for that bracket. It is useful for rough evaluation of deductions, income deferral, or income allocation.
- `Tax owed at top of bracket` is shown as `federal / Ontario / combined` and is calculated cumulatively by bracket. It does not include any `non-refundable tax credits`, `Ontario surtax`, or `Ontario Health Premium`.
- `Ontario surtax` depends on `Ontario basic tax payable` after allowable Ontario personal tax credits, so the taxable-income point where surtax starts is not identical for every filer.
- If we ignore credits and keep the same simplifying assumption used in the main table, surtax would begin at about `$86,102` of taxable income and the second surtax threshold would be reached at about `$103,555`.
- Final tax payable also depends on credits, deductions, income type, and family situation.
