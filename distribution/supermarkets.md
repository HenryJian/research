# Ontario Shiitake Buyer Leads: Supermarkets and Grocery Retailers

Checked on April 28, 2026 using public supermarket websites, online grocery product pages, store locator pages, supplier/vendor pages, and existing farm-retail research in this repository.

This file lists potential buyers for Ontario-grown shiitake mushrooms that are also supermarkets or grocery retailers with Ontario stores. It excludes pure distributors and wholesalers, which belong in [distributors.md](distributors.md), and mushroom farms or grower-wholesalers, which belong in [competitors.md](competitors.md).

Move note: Lococo's was moved here from [distributors.md](distributors.md). It has a wholesale arm, but the business is also a Southern Ontario grocery retailer with public retail stores, so it fits better in this supermarket buyer file.

Certification note: The "SQL certification" request is treated here as SQF certification, the grocery food-safety certification. Most retailers do not publicly say "SQF only"; they usually ask for an acceptable food-safety audit or a GFSI-recognized certification. SQF is GFSI-benchmarked ([SQFI source](https://www.sqfi.com/our-program/sqf-is-a-gfsi-standard)); CanadaGAP options B, C, and D are also GFSI-recognized for relevant fresh-produce scopes ([CanadaGAP source](https://www.canadagap.ca/certification/certification-options/)). CanadaGAP's current production/packing/storage commodity scope should be confirmed for a shiitake grower before assuming it is available for our exact operation ([CanadaGAP scope](https://www.canadagap.ca/faq-items/what-commodities-does-the-canadagap-program-currently-cover-2/)).

## How To Use This List

- High priority means the retailer has clear public shiitake or specialty mushroom evidence and a plausible Ontario buyer path.
- Medium priority means the format is a good fit, but either live shiitake evidence or the supplier route needs confirmation.
- Large-banner supermarkets may require retail-ready packs, UPCs, insurance, food-safety paperwork, reliable weekly volume, and category-manager approval.
- Independent and specialty stores may be more practical first tests for a Waterford / Brantford-area farm because they can sometimes trial a local product before a chain-wide listing.

## Notes

- Lococo's is no longer tracked in [distributors.md](distributors.md) to avoid double-counting. Use this file when treating it as a supermarket / retail grocery prospect.
- Nizam Produce is tracked here rather than in [distributors.md](distributors.md) because its public site describes it as a grocery store, even though it also says it does wholesale restaurant delivery.
- No other clear supermarket operators were moved from [distributors.md](distributors.md). CJR, Don's Produce, Oishi / BuyMushroom.ca, Mike & Mike's / Fresh Direct, Fresh Start Foods, and similar entries are distributors, wholesalers, marketplaces, or channel partners rather than supermarket retailers.
- For supermarket outreach, prepare a one-page sell sheet before calling: farm location, varieties, pack sizes, wholesale price, case size, weekly availability, delivery radius, food-safety documentation, insurance, certifications, shelf-life guidance, and whether the product is organic.
- For nearby independent or specialty stores, ask for a produce-manager trial first. For chains, ask whether local produce is handled at store, regional, banner, or corporate category-manager level.
- Public online grocery prices and stock status change frequently. Treat product pages as demand signals, not guaranteed current shelf placement at every Ontario store.
