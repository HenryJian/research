# Ontario Restaurant Market Size for Shiitake and Oyster Mushrooms

Checked on 2026-04-16.

## Bottom Line

I could not find a published Ontario statistic that directly reports restaurant purchases of shiitake or oyster mushrooms in pounds. The public data is good on total mushroom production, restaurant sales, and menu presence, but not on Ontario restaurant purchases by species.

My best working estimate for **fresh-weight annual consumption by Ontario restaurants** is:

| Mushroom | Working estimate |             Cautious range | Confidence    |
| -------- | ---------------: | -------------------------: | ------------- |
| Shiitake | ~600,000 lb/year | 500,000 to 700,000 lb/year | Medium        |
| Oyster   | ~125,000 lb/year | 100,000 to 150,000 lb/year | Low-to-medium |
| Combined | ~725,000 lb/year | 600,000 to 850,000 lb/year | Low-to-medium |

These figures should be treated as **market-sizing estimates**, not audited purchase totals.

## Why This Estimate Is Reasonable

### 1. There is no direct Ontario restaurant purchase dataset by mushroom species

- Statistics Canada publishes total mushroom production and provincial restaurant sales, but not restaurant purchases of shiitake or oyster by province.
- Mushroom Council / Circana foodservice reports are useful directional inputs, but they are U.S.-based and explicitly exclude a meaningful share of specialty-wholesaler business.

### 2. Ontario is the largest mushroom-producing province in Canada

- Statistics Canada reported that Ontario accounted for **51.8% of Canadian mushroom production in 2024**, with **84,871 short tons** sold in the province.
- Source: [Statistics Canada, Mushroom Growers' Survey, 2024](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/250625/dq250625e-eng.htm)

### 3. One Ontario specialty grower alone is already large enough to imply a meaningful restaurant market

- Holburne Mushroom Farm says it produces about **25,000 lb/week of shiitake** and **5,000 lb/week of oyster**, or roughly **1.30 million lb/year of shiitake** and **260,000 lb/year of oyster**.
- Holburne also says it sells to **foodservice and retail throughout Canada**.
- Sources:
  - [Holburne Mushroom Farm - Our Farm](https://www.holburnemushroom.ca/our-farm)
  - [Holburne Mushroom Farm - Foodservice & Retail](https://www.holburnemushroom.ca/foodservice-retail)

### 4. Restaurants are a major shiitake sales channel

- Cornell Small Farms reports that, in a Northeast shiitake grower study, **46% of mushrooms were sold to restaurants**, making restaurants the largest sales channel in that sample.
- The same Cornell summary cites a Chatham University buyer study where **23 buyers needed 7,075 lb/year of shiitake**, or about **307 lb per buyer per year**.
- Sources:
  - [Cornell Small Farms - Shiitake Mushrooms Turning a Profit for Forest Farmers in the Northeast](https://smallfarms.cornell.edu/2015/01/shiitakemushroomprofit/)
  - [Cornell Small Farms - Economic Report for Log-Grown Shiitake Mushrooms (2019)](https://smallfarms.cornell.edu/2022/02/economic-report-for-log-grown-shiitake-mushrooms-2019/)

### 5. Ontario is a very large share of Canadian restaurant demand

- Statistics Canada reported **December 2025** foodservice and drinking place sales of **$3.3945 billion in Ontario** versus **$8.6 billion for Canada**, so Ontario represented about **39.5%** of national foodservice sales that month.
- I used that 39.5% share as a practical proxy for Ontario's weight in national restaurant demand.
- Source: [Statistics Canada - Food services and drinking places, December 2025](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/daily-quotidien/260225/dq260225d-eng.htm)

### 6. Shiitake is clearly a larger restaurant market than oyster

- The Mushroom Council / Datassential report shows **shiitake on 10.1% of U.S. restaurant menus** versus **oyster on 1.5%**.
- The Circana broadline summary also shows shiitake sold mainly in **48 oz and 80 oz** foodservice packs, which is consistent with regular chef purchasing rather than only occasional novelty use.
- Sources:
  - [Mushroom Council - Specialty Mushrooms in Foodservice (Summer 2023)](https://www.mushroomcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/Specialty-Mushroom-Trends-in-Foodservice-Summer-2023.pdf)
  - [Mushroom Council - Fresh Mushroom Foodservice Performance (April 2024)](https://www.mushroomcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/2023_SupplyTrack_Fresh-Mushrooms-Foodservice-Performance.pdf)

## Calculation Floor

Using only Holburne's published production, the Cornell 46% restaurant-channel share, and Ontario's 39.5% share of Canadian foodservice sales gives a useful **floor** for Ontario restaurant demand:

- **Shiitake floor**: `25,000 lb/week x 52 x 46% x 39.5% = about 236,000 lb/year`
- **Oyster floor**: `5,000 lb/week x 52 x 46% x 39.5% = about 47,000 lb/year`

This is not a full-market estimate. It is only a floor implied by:

- one Ontario grower,
- one restaurant-channel benchmark,
- and Ontario's share of national foodservice demand.

Because Holburne sells across Canada and because Ontario also has other public shiitake/oyster competitors and suppliers such as Carleton Mushroom Farms, Silver Lake Mushrooms, Champion Mushrooms Farm, and Nebo Mushrooms, the true Ontario restaurant market should be materially above that floor.

## Working Estimate Logic

I therefore treat the Holburne-based calculation as a floor and widen it upward based on:

- Ontario's large share of Canadian restaurant spending,
- the presence of multiple other Ontario specialty-mushroom suppliers,
- shiitake's much higher menu penetration than oyster,
- and Cornell's buyer benchmark of about 307 lb/year per shiitake buyer.

That produces the following practical planning ranges:

- **Shiitake**: about **500,000 to 700,000 lb/year**
- **Oyster**: about **100,000 to 150,000 lb/year**

For farm-planning purposes, the midpoint working assumption is:

- **Shiitake**: **600,000 lb/year**
- **Oyster**: **125,000 lb/year**

## What This Means Operationally

- The Ontario restaurant market for **shiitake** looks large enough to support multiple serious farm-scale suppliers.
- The **oyster** restaurant market is smaller, but still appears to be a meaningful six-figure-pound opportunity province-wide.
- Shiitake appears to be the better species for a broad Ontario restaurant base; oyster looks more chef-driven and likely more concentrated in urban, higher-end, or plant-forward accounts.

## Supermarket Versus Restaurant Split

I could not find an **Ontario-specific** mushroom split between supermarket purchases and restaurant purchases.

The best direct mushroom-channel figure I found is from the USDA Economic Research Service. Using U.S. food-intake survey data available at the time, it reported:

- **Fresh-market mushrooms**: about **59%** purchased at retail / for at-home use, and about **42%** obtained from away-from-home sources.
- **Processed mushrooms**: about **79%** purchased at retail / for at-home use, and about **22%** obtained from away-from-home sources.

Sources:

- [USDA ERS - Factors Affecting U.S. Mushroom Consumption](https://www.ers.usda.gov/publications/pub-details?pubid=39490)
- [USDA ERS PDF mirror with the detailed percentages](https://downloads.usda.library.cornell.edu/usda-esmis/files/1n79h429p/qj72p9713/dr26z092h/VGS-03-31-2003_Special_Report.pdf)

For a more current **fresh mushroom** directional estimate, newer Mushroom Council / Circana data suggests something similar:

- Retail sold **324.4 million lb** of fresh mushrooms through traditional retail channels in the 52 weeks ending **November 2, 2025**.
- Foodservice broadline distributors sold **91.3 million lb** in **2023**, and that report says broadline covers about **41% of all foodservice distribution**.
- Grossing that up implies about **222.7 million lb** across total foodservice distribution.
- In that foodservice report, **full-service restaurants plus quick-service restaurants accounted for 83.7% of foodservice mushroom pounds**, implying about **186.4 million lb** moving through restaurants specifically.

That yields an approximate current U.S. fresh-market split of:

- **Retail / supermarket side**: about **63.5%**
- **Restaurants**: about **36.5%**

If non-restaurant foodservice is included as well, the split is closer to:

- **Retail**: about **59.3%**
- **All foodservice**: about **40.7%**

Sources:

- [Mushroom Council - Specialty Mushroom Sales and Performance Review 2025](https://www.mushroomcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Specialty-Mushroom-Sales-and-Performance-Review-2025.pdf)
- [Mushroom Council - Fresh Mushroom Foodservice Performance (April 2024)](https://www.mushroomcouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/2023_SupplyTrack_Fresh-Mushrooms-Foodservice-Performance.pdf)

## Caveats

- This estimate is for **fresh-weight restaurant consumption**, not retail sales and not dried-mushroom use.
- Some restaurant demand is met through importers and specialty distributors, which are not fully visible in public data.
- The Cornell restaurant-channel share is not Ontario-specific and comes from a Northeast U.S. shiitake context, so it should be treated as a directional benchmark.
- The Datassential / Mushroom Council menu data is U.S.-based, not Ontario-specific, and is used here only as a proxy for relative species popularity.
